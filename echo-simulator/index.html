Methodology & validation

Motion is real, not hand-drawn. Each loop's wall motion comes from cardiac cine-MRI (the public ACDC dataset, 5 diagnostic groups). Per-AHA-segment endocardial shortening is extracted from the end-diastolic and end-systolic segmentations and transferred onto a regularized biventricular template — real motion on clean anatomy. Atrial and PLAX geometry are resliced from whole-heart MRI (MMWHS).

The numbers are measured, never typed in. Ejection fraction is computed from the moving contour by single-plane method-of-discs (Simpson); RV:LV ratio, TAPSE and the PSAX septal index (D-sign) are likewise read off the deforming model. Changing a preset changes the geometry/motion; the readouts follow.

Presets. Normal; LAD/RCA/LCx infarcts (coronary→AHA territory map, with akinesis depth and transmural gradient calibrated from the real ACDC infarct cohort); RV strain (D-sign + reduced TAPSE); DCM and HCM are driven by individual real patients (ACDC patient017 and patient030).

Appearance. A labeled teaching view and a procedural grayscale B-mode view (correlated point-spread-function speckle, time-gain compensation, specular borders). The B-mode look is scored two ways against real echo: an image-statistics fidelity metric, and a round-trip through a real-echo segmentation network (CAMUS-trained nnU-Net) that recovers the LV from the rendered frame — confirming it is legible as echo, not just plausible to the eye.

Photoreal (beta). A third appearance runs each echo frame through a CycleGAN trained to map our render to real-echo texture (unpaired render↔echo). It is experimental: the texture is convincing but it is not gated for pathology fidelity, so a disease may read milder than it is — the displayed measurements still come from the validated model. Teaching/Echo remain the reference appearances.

Teaching tool — not a diagnostic reference. Pathology presets await formal cardiologist sign-off; Photoreal (beta) is an unvalidated neural rendering.
