Point-of-Care Ultrasound · Perioperative Medicine

POCUS Learning Case Series

Simulated teaching cases for anesthesiology residents

4 cases available 1 in development 2 interactive 3D tools

Each case follows a single simulated patient through a two-part clinical scenario, with bullet-point answer keys, original diagrams, and a grounding in current evidence. Where guidelines are evolving or sparse, the cases say so. Click any case to open it.

The cases

1 Gastric POCUS · Airway

Gastric Ultrasound & the Patient on a GLP-1 Receptor Agonist

Prevalence and theoretical risk of GLP-1 RAs, gastroparesis risk factors, ASA/CAS guidance, gastric grading, the discordance between high-risk grade and clinical aspiration, risk-mitigation strategies, and management of an aspiration event.

2-part scenario Open case →
2 Diaphragm POCUS · Phrenic nerve

Diaphragm POCUS & First Rib Resection for Thoracic Outlet Syndrome

Phrenic nerve injury, diaphragm excursion and thickening-fraction cutoffs, PACU crisis management, and long-term management of phrenic palsy including reconstruction-before-plication sequencing.

2-part scenario Open case →
3 Neuro POCUS · Neuraxial

Optic Nerve Sheath Diameter & Post-Dural Puncture Headache

Postpartum headache differential and incidence, the anatomical basis and bedside technique of ONSD, an honest appraisal of the evidence for detecting intracranial hypotension, and the 2023-consensus management of PDPH including the epidural blood patch.

2-part scenario Open case →
4 Vascular Access POCUS

Systematic Vascular Access in the Difficult, Edematous Patient

A systematic, ultrasound-guided approach to peripheral access when the patient is a difficult poke: DIVA risk-stratification, a RaPeVA-style survey with an interactive 3D arm and live ultrasound, vein selection and the 65% rule, then recognition and management of extravasation.

2-part scenario · interactive 3D Open case →
5 Airway POCUS

Role of Airway Ultrasound in Difficult Airway

Pre-intubation airway assessment, cricothyroid membrane identification, tracheal midline confirmation, and ultrasound-guided management of the unanticipated difficult airway.

About this series

Created by Raphael Lessard, MD, University of Ottawa.

The author thanks the Department of Anesthesiology and Pain Medicine at The Ottawa Hospital and its dedicated POCUS staff for their support in developing this series. Special acknowledgment to Dr. E. Miller and Dr. M. Eissa for their unwavering support and dedication.

These cases are designed for small-group or self-directed resident teaching. Answer keys are collapsible in the HTML versions, so learners can attempt each question before opening the answer key, and every case can be printed to PDF from the browser.

The perioperative evidence base — particularly for GLP-1 receptor agonists — is consensus-driven and changing quickly. Verify against your institution's current policy and the latest society guidance before applying any of this to patient care.
