Interactive Tools

Explore the anatomy alongside the ultrasound, at your own pace

3 tools available Orbit · zoom · click to explore

Standalone, self-contained 3D explorables that pair a rotatable anatomical model with the live ultrasound it produces. Drop in a scan plane, click a structure to learn its role and sonographic appearance, and watch the beating model line up with the image. Best experienced on a desktop browser with a mouse or trackpad.

The tools

3D Echocardiography · TTE

Interactive 3D Heart & Live Echo

Orbit a real anatomical heart and click any chamber, valve, or vessel. Toggle the four standard transthoracic windows — PLAX, PSAX, apical-4, and subcostal — to drop each scan plane into the heart beside the matching live ultrasound loop, with a beating-heart animation and flow coloring.

Interactive 3D · live echo Open tool →
3D Vascular Access · RaPeVA

3D Arm & Live Ultrasound

Run a systematic RaPeVA survey of the upper-limb veins: rotate the 3D arm, slide the probe along the vessels, and read the corresponding live ultrasound. Built to teach vein selection and the 65% depth rule for difficult IV access.

♥ Echocardiography · Pathology

EchoSimulator — Parametric Pathology Echo

Distinct from the 3D-heart anatomy tool: a parametric pathology simulator. Explore A4C, PSAX and PLAX loops across regional wall-motion abnormalities (LAD / RCA / LCx infarcts), RV strain, dilated (DCM) and hypertrophic (HCM) cardiomyopathy — all driven by real cardiac-MRI motion. EF, RV:LV, TAPSE and the D-sign are measured from the moving image, with a labeled teaching overlay or a grayscale B-mode view.

Before you launch

These tools render in 3D using WebGL and are best viewed on a desktop or laptop browser. They are schematic teaching models — proportions and positions are stylized for clarity and are not a substitute for scanning real patients.
